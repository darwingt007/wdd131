Site Plan: Healthy Cycling Life

Site Name

Healthy Cycling Life

The name **Healthy Cycling Life** clearly communicates the site's focus: promoting cycling as a sustainable habit that improves overall lifestyle, physical health, and mental well-being.

*Optional domain availability: healthycyclinglife.info*

---

Site Purpose

The purpose of this site is to provide comprehensive information on how cycling can improve lifestyle, physical and mental health, and overall **quality of life**. The site will detail **expected progress** users can see over time. Additionally, it will briefly direct users on how to **improve cycling performance** for those looking to take their riding further.

---

Scenarios (Target Audience Questions)

These questions represent a visitor's needs and will drive the site's content:

  • **What tangible improvements can I expect in my quality of life after three months of cycling?** (Targeting a beginner looking for long-term benefits)
  • **How does cycling specifically affect mental health and stress reduction?** (Targeting a visitor focused on mental wellness)
  • **I cycle regularly—what simple techniques can I use to improve my speed and endurance?** (Targeting the performance-focused visitor)
  • What type of cycling is best for beginners wanting to improve fitness (road, mountain, hybrid)?
---

Color Schema

The chosen color scheme is inspired by nature and the sport of cycling. All colors are **WCAG AAA compliant** for maximum accessibility.

  • Primary Color – Deep Forest Blue (#002E39):
    Used for the **header**, **footer**, and **section titles (h1, h2, h3)**.
  • Secondary Color – Green (#006400):
    Used for **buttons**, key **highlights**, and **accents**.
  • Background – Light Gray (#F5F5F5):
    Used for the general **page background**.
  • Text – Dark Gray (#333333):
    Used for all **body text** and paragraphs.
---

Typography

The site will use a single, highly readable font family across all elements:

Font: Arial / Helvetica / Sans-serif

Used for **all text** (headings, body, and navigation) for clean readability and universal compatibility.
